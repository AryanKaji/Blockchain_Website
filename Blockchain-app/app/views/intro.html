Blockchain is a revolutionary technology that enables secure and transparent data recording and transfer without the need for a central authority. It is essentially a decentralized ledger composed of blocks, where each block contains data, a timestamp, a nonce, a cryptographic hash of its own content, and the hash of the previous block. This chaining mechanism ensures immutability, meaning once data is recorded, it cannot be altered without affecting all subsequent blocks â€” a nearly impossible task due to the cryptographic security involved.

The first block in any blockchain is known as the Genesis Block. Unlike other blocks, the Genesis Block has no previous hash value and is hardcoded into the blockchain structure. Each new block that is added to the chain must reference the hash of the previous block, ensuring the continuity and integrity of the chain.

Blockchain is widely known as the underlying technology behind cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, but its application extends far beyond digital currencies. It is now being utilized in fields such as finance, supply chain, healthcare, voting systems, and more due to its transparency, security, and trustless nature. The ability to maintain a shared and immutable ledger makes it ideal for industries requiring auditability and data consistency.
